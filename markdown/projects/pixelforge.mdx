This project uses satellite remote sensing to estimate water quality parameters—Dissolved Oxygen (DO), Total Dissolved Solids (TDS), and pH—from optical reflectance data. By applying the Normalized Difference Water Index (NDWI) to multispectral satellite images, the study establishes correlations between satellite-derived indices and ground-measured water quality values. The goal is to create a rapid, low-cost, non-invasive method for water monitoring, especially useful for urban lakes
